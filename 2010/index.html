2012-2013 Season

Wolsey Orchestra, a non-profit making charity, was founded in 1972 as a chamber orchestra. Committed to promoting concerts of the highest musical standards, it is now a full symphony orchestra in the forefront of orchestral music in Suffolk. The tradition of imaginative programming brings both new commissions and large-scale landmark works to Suffolk audiences. Working solely with professional conductors, and offering a concerto platform to locally born musicians, Wolsey Orchestra is one of the finest amateur orchestras in East Anglia.

40th Birthday Concert

Saturday 15th October 2012
Ipswich Corn Exchange
Conductor: Ben Palmer
Ben Palmer - Fanfare: Wolsey Orchestra 40th birthday commission
Host - Ballet: The Perfect Fool
Dvorak - Carnival Overture
Tchaikovsky - Symphony No 5 in E Minor
A trumpeter and composer by training, Ben, who grew up in Bury St Edmunds, studied music at the University of Birmingham, graduating with first class honours in 2003. He stayed on at Birmingham to complete an MPhil in composition, before moving to London in 2005 to study at the Royal Academy of Music with Simon Bainbridge. Increasingly in demand as an inspiring and versatile conductor, plans for 2013 include his debut at the Queen Elizabeth Hall and returns to Purcell Room and St John?s, Smith Square, London.
Saturday 9th February 2013
The Apex, Charter Square, Arc, Bury St Edmunds
Conductor: Andrew Morley
Soloist: Sally Beck
Ravel - Alborada Del Gracioso
Khachaturian - Flute Concerto
Tchaikovsky - Capriccio Italien
Respighi - The Pines of Rome
Sally Beck comes from the Bury St Edmunds area and began learning the flute aged 6, subsequently performing with Suffolk Youth Orchestra and the National Youth Orchestra of Gt. Britain, before winning a scholarship to the Royal Academy of Music where she won both the Wind and Concerto prizes. After further studies both on a Fellowship to Los Angeles and in Banff, Canada, she studied for her Master?s Degree at the Hochschule für Musik und Tanz, Köln. Sally is a busy freelance musician and teacher in Cologne and was a major prize-winner in the Alexander Buono International Flute Competition.
Saturday 11th May 2013
Ipswich Corn Exchange
Conductor: Alexander Walker
Soloists: Rachel Helleur - Cello, Anna Helleur - Violin
Beethoven - Overture: Leonora No 3.
Brahms - Double Concerto in A Minor Op. 102
Dvorak - Symphony No 7 in D
Anna and Rachel Helleur grew up in Ipswich, both beginning violin lessons at the age of 3 with Clare Santer and the Ipswich Suzuki Group, Rachel then changing to cello aged 6.
Anna studied at Chetham's School of Music, participating in masterclasses with Yehudi Menuhin, Zakhar Bron and Emmanuel Hurwitz and winning the Chetham's Concerto Competition at the age of 16. Anna continued her studies in the U.S.A. and won a scholarship to the Aspen Music School in Colorado. She has worked with conductors such as David Zinman, Lawrence Foster, Stephen Kovacevich and Kent Nagano whilst a member of the Aspen Festival Orchestra and the Britten-Pears Orchestra and has, briefly, been a session-player in Hollywood.
Rachel studied at the Purcell School of Music and the Royal Academy of Music, taking a semester's exchange to the Eastman School of Music in the USA. Moving to Berlin in 2004 to study at the Hochschule fuer Musik 'Hanns Eisler' she later took up a place at the Orchestral Academy of the Berlin Philharmonic. Rachel gained orchestral experience as guest principal cello with the Bavarian Radio Orchestra, the Hamburg Radio Orchestra, the London Philharmonic Orchestra and the Philharmonia Orchestra. In 2007 Rachel was engaged as Principal Cellist of the Deutsche Oper in Berlin, then 2 years later became a member of the world-renowned Berliner Philharmoniker.
Anna and Rachel are thrilled to be performing Brahms glorious final orchestral masterpiece in their home town.
