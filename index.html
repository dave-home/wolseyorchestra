Our Next Concert

Saturday 11th October 2025 at 7:30pm
The Corn Exchange, King Street, Ipswich, IP1 1DH
Conductor - Dexter Drown
Leader - Dan Kent
Stravinsky: Petrushka (1947 version)
John Williams: Harry Potter Symphonic Suite
Mussorgsky: Pictures at an Exhibition (orch. Ravel)

Following on from our hugely successful concert in the Ipswich Corn Exchange in Autumn 2024, the Wolsey Orchestra are delighted to return to present another exciting evening of orchestral favourites. On this occasion, we will be conducted by the up-and-coming young conductor Dexter Drown - making his first concert appearance in Ipswich.

Mussorgsky's Pictures at an Exhibition - originally composed as a piano suite in 10 movements and depicting a tour of an art exhibition - is widely consider to be one of Mussorgsky's greatest works. However it is the stunning orchestration of the suite by Ravel nearly half a century later, in 1922, that is more widely known and loved by audiences today. Petrushka is one of three ground-breaking ballets composed by a young Stravinsky just before the outbreak of WW1 at the request of the impresario Diaghilev, and was first performed by the Ballet Russe. Stravinsky's 1947 reorchestration of the original ballet music is a real-de-force of the orchestral repertoire

And finally to bring the music up to the current day we will perform a work by the master of film music John Williams - his Harry Potter Symphonic Suite. We shall certainly be enjoying performing all three items for you, so do book your tickets early!
