Our Next Concert

Our next concert is a very special event for the Wolsey Orchestra.

On Sunday 29th October 1972 a group of young amateur musicians staged a concert in the Town Hall, Ipswich - and the Wolsey Chamber Orchestra was born. Following on from that initial event, the newly-formed orchestra presented further concerts in Town Hall, usually two or three per year, and these concerts soon became a regular feature of classical music making in Ipswich. Over the years, although the orchestra grew in size putting on full scale symphonic (rather than chamber) works and moved into the larger Corn Exchange Grand Hall, it retained the original idea of engaging professional conductors and young professional soloists to present concerts that were both challenging for the local musicians and yet of a high-standard of music making for the discerning local audiences. Apart from an enforced break as a result of the recent COVID epedemic, the Wolsey Orchestra has now been presenting classical concerts to Ipswich audiences for 50 years.

Our next concert will take place on Saturday 8th October 2022 at the Royal Hospital School, Holbrook and will be conducted by the Andrew Morley. It will feature two of the pieces from that original concert back in 1972, namely the Lark Ascending by Vaughan Williams and Introduction and Rondo Capriccioso by Saint‑Saëns - both featuring the brilliant young violinist Julia Hwang. The concert will also contain performances of the Peer Gynt Suite No.1 by Grieg and the Symphony No.9 by Shostakovich.
