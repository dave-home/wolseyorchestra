Our Next Concert

The Wolsey Orchestra is delighted to be back after COVID and our members are really excited to be performing again in public. We hope that all our regular followers will come and support us as we bring live classical music back to the Ipswich audience.
 
We regret to announce that Emiliano Ramniceanu, who was originally engaged as the soloist for the Grieg Piano Concerto, has been unable to obtain a visa to come to the UK to perform with us, as a result of the surge in visa applications following the conflict in Ukraine. We are still hoping that Emiliano will be able to come to Ipswich to play a concerto with the orchestra at some stage in the future. In the meantime, we are delighted to welcome the versatile young pianist Jennifer Carter who has agreed, at short notice, to perform the Grieg concerto with us.

Saturday 14th May 2022 at 7:30pm
Royal Hospital School Concert Hall
Conductor - Richard Laing
Leader - Nick Reynolds
Soloist - Jennifer Carter
Mendelssohn: Overture - Ruy Blas
Grieg: Piano Concerto in A minor, Op.16
Dvořák: Symphony No.7 in D minor, Op.70

For our first concert after the COVID pandemic, the Wolsey Orchestra is featuring one of the most well-known piano concertos in the repertoire - Grieg's ever-popular Piano Concerto in A minor. Much in demand as an accompanist, chamber musician, orchestral pianist and soloist, we are delighted that Jennifer Carter has been able to find time to come and perform with the Wolsey Orchestra here in Ipswich. In the second half of the concert we will treat you to a performance of Dvořák's delightful Symphony No.7. Written and first performed in 1885 in London, it is recognised (along with Brahms' four symphonies and Schubert's 9th symphony) as "among the greatest and purest examples of the art form since Beethoven". And to start the evening's programme, we have the overture to Ruy Blas by Mendelssohn.
